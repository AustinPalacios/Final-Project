Donna Tartt

Donna Tart is an American author, whose novels are The Secret History, The Little Friend, and The Goldfinch. Tartt won the WH Smith Literary Award for the The Little Friend in 2003 and the Pulitzer Prize for The Goldfinch in 2014. Tartt was also included in Time magazine's 2014 "100 Most Influential People" list. While Tartt is mostly known for her Fiction writing, she has also written many short stories that has been included in the Best American Short Stories.

Jane Austen

Jane Austen was an English novelist who was known for her critique and comment upon the British landed gentry. Austen's novels often focused on exploring the dependence of women on marriage in the pursuit of favourable social and economic security. Austen's most famous novels were Sense and Sensibility, Pride and Prejudice, Mansfield Park, and Emma. Funnily enough though, Austen gained majority of her fame after she passed, and her six full-length novels have rarely been out of print. Austen has inspired a large number of critical essays and literary anthologies.

"If I loved you less, I might be able to talk about it more."- Jane Austen, Emma

Sarah J. Maas

Sarah J. Maas is an American fantasy author who began writing her first,and debut, novel at the age of sixteen. Maas is the #1 New York Times and internationally bestselling author of the Throne of Glass, Court of Thorns and Roses, and Crescent City series. Her books have sold millions of copies and are published in thirty-seven languages. Maas's most recent series,Crescent City, is ranked one of the top twenty Science Fiction and Fantasy books of 2020.

"I can survive well enough on my own- if given the proper reading material"- Sarah J. Maas, Throne of Glass

Virginia Woolf

Virginia Woolf was an English writer and is considered one of the most important modernist authors. Woolf pioneered the use of the stream of consciousness as a narative device and became one of the central subjects of the 1970s movement of feminist criticism, and her works have since attracted much attention and widespread commentary for "inspiring feminism". Woolf's most famous novels are Mrs Dalloway, To the Lighthouse and Orlando, many of which have been translated into more than fifty languages. Woolf is commemorated today by statues, societies dedicated to her work and a building at the University of London.

"Books are the mirrors of the soul"- Virginia Woolf, Between the Acts
J.K. Rowling

J.K. Rowling is an British author who is the genius behind the multiple award winning Harry Potter series. Rowling is known as the best-selling children's book series in history and has sold more than five hundred million copies. She is known to be the first author to become a billionare. Though, that never stopped her to donating most of her earnings to charity. Rowling is the founder of the Volant Charitable Trust to at-risk woman, children and young people and has supported multiple charities.
